# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fairpicture internal staff (editors, project managers, compliance leads) working at a desk, in a browser, during working hours. They are reviewing batches of field photography and need to verify that every image of an identifiable person has a matching signed consent PDF. They trust the tool to surface correct matches and flag gaps; they make the final call.

## Product Purpose

CAM (Consent Asset Matcher) is an internal compliance tool that connects Canto DAM images to their consent PDFs. It scans projects, scores image-to-PDF matches using name, project, and location signals, and tracks consent coverage across the entire photo library. Success means every image that needs a consent has one confirmed in the system — and the team can prove it.

## Brand Personality

Precise, honest, purposeful. CAM handles sensitive data about real people; it should feel rigorous and trustworthy. It is part of the Fairpicture product family and shares its red-on-white visual identity.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (Mixpanel, Segment): blue gradient heroes, funnel charts, the hero-metric template with big numbers and gradient accents.
- NGO / charity websites: photography-heavy warm layouts that feel like donation pages.
- AI startup products: glassmorphism, purple gradients, "magical" motion language.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** Fairpicture's product is ethical image use. CAM's UI should reflect that seriousness — no decorative imagery, no stock-photo vibes, no performative warmth.
2. **Signal over noise.** Every element earns its place by communicating state, status, or action. If it doesn't inform a decision, remove it.
3. **Consent is binary.** The UI must make it instantly clear whether an image has a confirmed consent or not. Ambiguity in the UI reflects ambiguity in compliance.
4. **Fairpicture red owns the brand seam.** The 1px red divider on header and sidebar edges is the fingerprint; everything else is neutral.
5. **Tools, not experiences.** No onboarding drama, no delight animations, no personality-for-personality's-sake. Get the editor to the match and out again.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Internal tool but used by staff with varying assistive technology needs. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements, proper ARIA roles on dynamic table content, sufficient contrast on all text.
